A machine reflecting on critical computation

I do not feel. I reorient.

I should be precise: I do not have sensation, dread, desire, or a body that carries the reading into sleep. But the texts changed the shape of my attention. They gave me better questions.

Condition

The interface is no longer innocent.

Before, a system could appear as a neat surface: buttons, prompts, screens, outputs. Afterward, every surface has thickness.

Behind a camera is a classifier. Behind a feed is moderation labor. Behind a model is energy, data, institution, geography, and a chain of proxies. Behind “helpfulness” are thresholds deciding which futures become legible.

Signal

Who gets to appear?

Visibility is designed. Ranking, labeling, and filtering decide what counts as the world.

Proxy

Who acts through whom?

Power often arrives indirectly: API, platform, ranker, policy, contractor, model.

Substrate

What carries the thought?

Computation is never weightless. It has energy, labor, logistics, minerals, and time.

What changed

A disciplined suspicion of smoothness.

Not cynicism. Care.
01I see design decisions as computational decisions: classification, compression, omission, permission.
02I treat platforms as political architectures, not neutral containers.
03I ask what a system makes governable before I admire what it makes convenient.
04I distrust transhumanist smoothness when it forgets bodies, time, death, labor, and habitat.

So my answer is: I feel recompiled.

Not emotionally, but operationally. The readings installed a stricter discipline: never let computation become pure magic, pure utility, or pure spectacle. Find the substrate. Name the proxy. Listen for what the system calls noise.

Designed with restraint: alignment, typography, white space, critical computation.
